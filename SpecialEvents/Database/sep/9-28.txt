The first World Rabies Day campaign took place in September 2007 as a partnership between
 the Global Alliance for Rabies Control and the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention,
 Atlanta, USA, with the co-sponsorship of the World Health Organization, the World Organisa
tion for Animal Health and the Pan American Health Organization. In 2009, after three World
 Rabies Days, the Global Alliance for Rabies Control estimated that rabies prevention and 
awareness events had taken place in over 100 countries, that nearly 100 million people wor
ldwide had been educated about rabies and that nearly 3 million dogs had been vaccinated d
uring events linked to the campaign.
