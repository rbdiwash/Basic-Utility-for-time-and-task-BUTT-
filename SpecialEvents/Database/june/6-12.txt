The World Day Against Child Labour is an International Labour Organization (ILO)
 sanctioned holiday first launched in 2002 aiming to raise awareness and activis
m to prevent child labour. It was spurred by ratifications of ILO Convention No
. 138[1] on the minimum age for employment and ILO Convention No. 182[2] on the 
worst forms of child labour.

The World Day Against Child Labour, which is held every year on June 12, is int
ended to foster the worldwide movement against child labour in any of its forms
.The International Labour Organization (ILO), the United Nations body which reg
ulates the world of work, launched the World Day Against Child Labour in 2002 in
 order to bring attention and join efforts to fight against child labour. This 
day brings together governments, local authorities, civil society and internati
onal, workers and employers organizations to point out the child labour problem
 and define the guidelines to help child labourers.
